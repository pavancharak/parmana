\# Parmana Business Transaction Model v1 (Locked)



\## Status



\*\*Version:\*\* 1.0



\*\*Status:\*\* Locked



\---



\# Purpose



Parmana is \*\*Execution Trust Infrastructure\*\*.



The fundamental unit managed by Parmana is the \*\*Business Transaction\*\*.



Every request submitted to Parmana represents exactly one business transaction. All trust artifacts generated by Parmana belong to that transaction.



This document defines the canonical Business Transaction model for Parmana.



\---



\# Definition



A Business Transaction represents a unique business event that requires policy evaluation and trusted execution.



Examples include:



\* Approve Payment

\* Approve Loan

\* Create Customer

\* Cancel Order

\* Approve Insurance Claim



The Business Transaction is owned by the calling application.



Parmana never generates the Business Transaction identifier.



\---



\# Business Transaction Identifier



Every request \*\*must\*\* include:



```json

{

&#x20; "metadata": {

&#x20;   "businessTransactionId": "PAY-20260626-000123"

&#x20; }

}

```



The `businessTransactionId` is mandatory.



It uniquely identifies the business event throughout its lifecycle.



\---



\# Ownership



The Business Transaction ID is supplied by the client application.



Parmana treats it as an immutable external identifier.



Its purposes are:



\* Business traceability

\* Audit

\* Storage

\* Retrieval

\* Replay

\* Trust verification



\---



\# One Business Transaction = One Execution Trust Record



A Business Transaction has exactly one Execution Trust Record.



```text

Business Transaction

&#x20;       │

&#x20;       ▼

Execution Trust Record

&#x20;       │

&#x20;       ├── Policy

&#x20;       ├── Signals

&#x20;       ├── Decision

&#x20;       ├── Execution

&#x20;       ├── Evidence

&#x20;       ├── Verification

&#x20;       ├── Receipt

&#x20;       └── Trust Chain

```



The Execution Trust Record represents the authoritative history of that business transaction.



\---



\# Execution Attempts



Execution retries do \*\*not\*\* create new Business Transactions.



Execution retries do \*\*not\*\* create new Execution Trust Records.



Instead, retries are recorded as execution attempts within the same Business Transaction.



```text

Business Transaction

│

├── Attempt 1

│      Failed

│

├── Attempt 2

│      Failed

│

└── Attempt 3

&#x20;      Success

```



This preserves a complete operational history while maintaining a single authoritative trust record.



\---



\# Immutable Trust Record



Once created, the Execution Trust Record is append-only.



New evidence may be appended.



New execution attempts may be appended.



Historical records are never modified or replaced.



\---



\# Business Outcome



A Business Transaction represents one business outcome.



Examples:



```text

PAY-1001

Approve Payment

```



Retrying the execution because of a temporary network failure does not create another business transaction.



It remains:



```text

Business Transaction

PAY-1001

```



Similarly:



```text

LOAN-2001

Approve Loan

```



always represents one loan approval transaction.



\---



\# Trust Artifacts



Every trust artifact belongs to the Business Transaction.



```text

Business Transaction

│

├── Metadata

├── Policy

├── Signals

├── Decision

├── Execution Attempts

├── Evidence

├── Verification

├── Receipt

└── Trust Chain

```



No trust artifact exists independently of a Business Transaction.



\---



\# API Resource Model



The Business Transaction is the primary resource exposed by Parmana.



Example API structure:



```text

POST /transactions



GET /transactions/{businessTransactionId}



GET /transactions/{businessTransactionId}/receipt



GET /transactions/{businessTransactionId}/trust-chain



POST /transactions/{businessTransactionId}/verify



POST /transactions/{businessTransactionId}/replay

```



The Business Transaction ID is the canonical identifier used throughout the API.



\---



\# Design Principles



\## Principle 1



Every request represents exactly one Business Transaction.



\---



\## Principle 2



The Business Transaction ID is mandatory.



\---



\## Principle 3



The Business Transaction ID is owned by the client application.



Parmana never generates or modifies it.



\---



\## Principle 4



One Business Transaction has exactly one Execution Trust Record.



\---



\## Principle 5



Execution retries are recorded as execution attempts within the same trust record.



They do not create additional execution records.



\---



\## Principle 6



All trust artifacts belong to the Business Transaction.



There are no standalone receipts, verifications, or trust chains outside the context of a Business Transaction.
