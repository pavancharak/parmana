\# Parmana Vision



Version: 1.0



\---



\# Vision



Artificial intelligence and software automation are increasingly making decisions and executing actions that affect businesses, customers, and society.



As organizations adopt autonomous systems, a fundamental question emerges:



\*\*How can we trust that automated systems execute only what they are authorized to do?\*\*



Parmana exists to answer that question.



Our vision is a world where every automated execution can be trusted, verified, and independently audited.



\---



\# Mission



Parmana provides \*\*Execution Trust Infrastructure\*\*.



It establishes a verifiable trust chain between business authorization, policy evaluation, execution, and execution evidence.



By producing verifiable execution artifacts, Parmana enables organizations to understand, verify, and audit what automated systems actually executed.



\---



\# The Problem



Modern software systems increasingly rely on:



\* Artificial Intelligence

\* Autonomous Agents

\* Workflow Automation

\* Event-Driven Systems

\* Microservices



While these systems improve efficiency, they also increase the difficulty of answering fundamental questions such as:



\* Who authorized this execution?

\* What business intent was approved?

\* Which policy governed the decision?

\* Why was this action executed?

\* Can the execution be independently verified?

\* Can the execution be replayed for audit or investigation?



Many existing systems focus on making decisions.



Far fewer focus on making execution trustworthy.



Parmana addresses this gap.



\---



\# Our Approach



Parmana treats execution as an explicit trust chain rather than a sequence of runtime events.



Each execution is linked to the business context that authorized it.



The execution lifecycle includes:



\* Authority

\* Authorization

\* Intent

\* Business Transaction

\* Policy Reference

\* Policy Evaluation

\* Decision

\* Execution

\* Execution Trust Record

\* Receipt

\* Verification

\* Replay



Each stage contributes to a verifiable execution history.



\---



\# Design Principles



Parmana is designed around the following principles.



\## Trust Before Automation



Automation should execute only after the required trust artifacts have been established.



\---



\## Explicit Policy



Every execution references an explicit business policy.



Runtime behavior should never depend on implicit policy discovery or version selection.



\---



\## Deterministic Evaluation



Given the same policy and runtime inputs, policy evaluation should produce consistent decision outcomes.



\---



\## Verifiable Evidence



Execution should produce evidence that can be independently verified.



\---



\## Separation of Responsibilities



Business authorization, policy evaluation, execution, evidence generation, verification, and replay are distinct responsibilities.



\---



\## Evidence Over Assertion



Technical claims should be supported by implementation, testing, audit evidence, and documented proofs.



\---



\# Long-Term Vision



Parmana aims to become the trust layer for automated execution across industries.



Rather than replacing existing applications or AI systems, Parmana provides the infrastructure that enables organizations to trust and verify their execution.



The long-term objective is to make verifiable execution a standard capability for autonomous and AI-driven systems, in the same way that encryption became a standard capability for secure communication.



\---



\# Success



Parmana succeeds when organizations can answer, with confidence:



\* What was authorized?

\* What policy governed the execution?

\* What decision was made?

\* What was executed?

\* What evidence was produced?

\* Can the execution be independently verified?



Execution trust should become a standard property of modern software systems.



\---



\# Scope



Parmana is not an AI model.



Parmana is not a workflow engine.



Parmana is not a policy authoring platform.



Parmana is execution trust infrastructure that provides verifiable execution evidence for automated systems.



\---



\# Guiding Principle



\*\*Organizations should not have to trust that automated systems executed correctly.\*\*



\*\*They should be able to verify it.\*\*
